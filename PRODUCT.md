# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People who have recently had a psychedelic or plant medicine experience — a retreat, ceremony, or guided session — and are now back in ordinary life feeling the afterglow fade. They came home changed, but daily life hasn't caught up. They feel the disconnect between who they were in the experience and who they are at the desk, in the relationship, in the routine. They are not beginners looking for their first experience. They are not clinical patients. They are adults — often high-functioning, thoughtful, spiritually curious — who had something profound happen and don't want to lose it.

Their context: overwhelmed by re-entry, living too much in their heads, feeling isolated because no one around them quite understands what they went through.

## Product Purpose

Somenta is a guided integration community. It gives post-retreat people a structured weekly rhythm — daily somatic audio drops, a live Wednesday regulation class, silent co-working practice rooms, and intimate 8-to-12 person share pods running in 12-week sprints — to help them translate their experience into lasting change.

The product is not therapy. It is not a meditation app. It is not a content library. It is a living, breathing container: consistent rhythm, real people, real practice. Success looks like someone feeling less scattered, more grounded, and genuinely connected to others who understand the path they're on.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, Warm, Intentional.

Voice: a trusted guide who has done the work themselves. Not clinical. Not hyped. Not a salesperson. The tone is like a wise friend who speaks plainly, holds space well, and never overpromises. Warmth without sentimentality. Structure without coldness. Depth without mysticism for its own sake.

Emotional goals: the visitor should feel immediately understood (someone here knows what this is like), gently held (this is a safe place), and quietly confident (this has a shape, a rhythm, a path forward).

## Anti-references

- **Generic meditation apps** (Calm, Headspace): pastel, consumer-wellness, gamified streaks, soft illustrations. Somenta is not self-help lite.
- **Clinical therapy platforms** (BetterHelp, therapy aggregators): cold, medical, transactional. Somenta is not a healthcare product.
- **Psychedelic hype brands**: neon, trippy visuals, AI-generated cosmic imagery, mystical typography. The work is sacred but Somenta doesn't perform sacredness.
- **SaaS landing page clichés**: hero + feature grid + pricing table, could be any software product. Somenta is not a productivity tool.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the container, not the content.** The design itself should feel like the space Somenta provides — grounded, unhurried, warm. Let the aesthetic do the emotional work before a single word is read.
2. **Earn trust through restraint.** Nothing shouty, nothing hyped, nothing that asks to be noticed. The rarity of emphasis is the emphasis.
3. **The body before the mind.** Somatic, sensory, textural cues over cognitive or analytical ones. Warmth comes from material choices, not copy.
4. **Community as evidence.** Real quotes and real names carry more weight than feature lists. Show the people, not the product.
5. **Stillness is a feature.** Whitespace and calm rhythm are not emptiness — they are the experience. An overcrowded page would betray the product.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA compliance. The audience includes people in emotionally sensitive periods of their life — avoid anything jarring, high-contrast aggressive, or motion-heavy that could feel destabilising. Prefer gentle transitions. Respect reduced-motion preferences.
